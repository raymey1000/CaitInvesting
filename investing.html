Caitlin Investing Tips
Should I get a high yield saving account?

A high yield checking account is not really that useful. I feel it's mainly a ploy to join the bank.

Getting a brokerage account is much more advantageous, a brokerage:

  • has money market funds with higher yield than bank accounts but can be accessed the same
  • has index funds of stocks with much higher return than bank accounts (10%, ~7% real)
  • has Roth IRA accounts with tax-free investing for tuition, first home and retirement
  • has access to bonds and bond funds which may be useful

You'd want the money in your checking account to stay roughly stable while the amount in the brokerage account grows over your life. Example: 2% in checking/CDs (expenses + emergency fund), 9% in house, 89% in brokerage.

If I open a brokerage account what should I invest in?

I'd say open a brokerage account, move a part of your savings over and invest in money market funds first. Vanguard offers a range of options, even the safe federal one is ~4% right now.

Allocation rule of thumb: the longer the goal and the more risk you can take, the more you put in stocks. For shorter term goals like house deposit or tuition, keep more in money markets.

For stocks a very common fund is VTSAX, which is diversified across all US stocks, has very low expense ratio, and a great return.

Another great option is Roth IRA investing.

How should I use a Roth IRA?
