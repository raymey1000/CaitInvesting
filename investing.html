Caitlin Investing Tips
Should I get a high yield saving account?

A high yield checking account is not really that useful. I feel it's mainly a ploy to join the bank.

Getting a brokerage account is much more advantageous, a brokerage:

  • has money market funds with higher yield than bank accounts but can be accessed the same
  • has index funds of stocks with much higher return than bank accounts (10%, ~7% real)
  • has Roth IRA accounts with tax-free investing for tuition, first home and retirement
  • has access to bonds and bond funds which may be useful

You'd want the money in your checking account to stay roughly stable while the amount in the brokerage account grows over your life. Example: 2% in checking/CDs, 9% in house, 89% in brokerage.

If I open a brokerage account what should I invest in?

I'd say open a brokerage account and move a part of your savings over and invest in money market funds first. Vanguard offers a range of options, even the safe federal one is ~4% right now.

Rule of thumb: the longer the goal and the more risk you can take, the more you put in stocks. For shorter-term goals like a house deposit or tuition, keep more in money markets.

For stocks a very common fund is VTSAX, diversified across all US stocks, with very low expense ratio and strong returns.

Another option is Roth IRA investing.

What about Bitcoin or Nvidia stock?

Cryptocurrency is very high risk and hard to understand. Individual stocks can have high gains but, like crypto, can go to zero. When you buy the total stock market (or S&P 500) they will never go to zero, and even during the Great Depression the market recovered.

There are two types of investor: passive vs active. Passive investing uses buy-and-hold, while active investors trade frequently. Passive investing is super easy, takes little time, and often has huge gains over time. In fact, passive investing often outperforms active investing.
